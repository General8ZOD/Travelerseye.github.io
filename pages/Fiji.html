Fiji


Fiji is a country in the south pacific ocean, located within the Oceania continent. It is approximately 4600 thousand miles from the coast to Australia, with a population of at least 890k people.

Cutural Facts

Languages Spoken: English, Fijian, and Hindi
Before traveling here, try to learn basic greetings in Fijian.
Dance Performances: Cultural 'meke' (all kinds of traditioanl song and dance)
Dances like graceful fan performances and war dances.
These perfomances would tell a story about theor history, love , or a legend.
Religion: Christianity,Islam, Hinduism, and Sikhism

Food


Photo by Andre Panerai via flickr

Fijians are known for their huge feast known as mekes where they cook food in underground earth oven called lovo where stones are used to heat it. A few dishes you can enjoy here are
  • Yam
  • Taro (A yam-like staple of Fijian Cuisine)
  • Kumala(Sweet potato)
  • Roti(flatbread)
  • Topi (Fijian dumplings)
  • Fish Suruwa (Fish curry)
  • Fiji chop suey
  • Cassava cake (traditional moist cake)

Tourist Hotspots

While visiting Fiji a few tourist hotspots you can check out are: the Mamanuca Islands, Garden of the Sleeping Giant, Qalito Island, Malolo, Fijis Museum, Tokoriki, Mana Island, Tavarua, and Kula Wild Adventure Park. In these hotspots, you can enjoy animal encounters, ziplinging, snorkeling, surfing, breath taking beaches,and diving.

Travel Cost


For a 3-star hotel you can expect to pay an average of $77.
For a 5-star hotel you can expect to pay an average of $380.
An average plane ticket can range from $670-$1075

Current Event

Covid-19: Due to the pandemic travel to Fiji is currently not open to none Fijian citizens. Arrival by plane or boat requires special approval by the Prime Minister. Once you have approval, upon arrival you have to quarantine for 14 days and receive regular screenings. Further information is located at Covid Entry Requirements
